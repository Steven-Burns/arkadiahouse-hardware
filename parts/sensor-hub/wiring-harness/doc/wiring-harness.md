# sensor hub wiring harness
Getting a bunch of shielded CAT6A cable (read: thick and stiff) into a 2-gang electrical box is a pain.  This thingy helps some.

Sheilded CAT6A was chosen because:
- It's cheap and widely available and well-understood.
- It's simple to standardize on a single type of cable.
- The sheilding seemed like a good idea from what I read about DMX crosstalk.  It can't hurt.  I'm not sophisticated enough to try to design around EMI.
- RJ45 connectors are relatively compact.  Naked wires in screw terminals also works fine.  The overall project uses both.
